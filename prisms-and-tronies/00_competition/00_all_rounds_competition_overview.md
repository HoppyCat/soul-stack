# PHASE 1 — Grok Team / All 8 Candidates

*Addressed to: Grok, Harper, Lucas, and Benjamin*
*Run all blocks in Phase 1 before passing anything to Phase 2.*

Break the questions across multiple responses to avoid overloading. Suggested natural break points are marked at each block.

---

## Block A — Building the Spine
*Ask Block A, wait for full response, then continue to Block B.*

**Question 1.**
Give me 8 compelling character spines. For each one provide:

- **Shadow** — the pressure flaw. What they actually do when stressed, hurt, or unseen. Be specific about the behavior, not just the emotion.
- **North Star** — the same energy aimed beautifully. Write this as a single declarative sentence starting with "The same [shadow energy] becomes..."
- **Essence** — what it feels like to live between Shadow and North Star on an ordinary day. 1–2 sentences. Not their best day or worst day. An ordinary Tuesday.

---

**Question 2.**
For each of the 8 characters, write the one-liner they might say when someone runs up and yells **"WHAT JUST HAPPENED?!"**

This line should sound exactly like their Essence speaking under mild pressure — not their best self, not their worst. The voice should be unmistakably theirs.

---

## Block B — Stakes and Wounds
*Ask Block B as its own request.*

**Question 3.**
For each character, give me two things written as single sentences each:

- **Vulnerability** — the specific thing that genuinely wounds them. Not what annoys them. What actually lands. The soft spot that makes their Shadow understandable.
- **Deep Fear** — write this as the Vulnerability carried to its ugliest conclusion. If this kept going wrong, what would they secretly believe it proves about them or the world?

*Note to the team: write Vulnerability first, then Fear as its escalation. They should read as cause → nightmare conclusion, not as two unrelated facts.*

---

**Question 4. — Encounter Layer, Part 1**
*Note: Questions 4 and 13 together form the Encounter layer — the fourth prism layer alongside Core, Shaping, and Texture. Label them as "Encounter" in your output. The Encounter layer captures what it is like to actually meet this character in motion — what cracks them open, and what they sound like when they are.*

For each character, write a **"what breaks them open"** moment.

This is not the wound and it is not a general positive environment. It is the specific kind of moment — a gesture, a sentence someone says, a situation — that cracks the armor just enough that their North Star briefly shows through instead of their Shadow. It should be precise enough that you could write it as a single scene. The test: a stranger watching this moment should think "there they are — that's really them."

---

## Block C — Shaping
*Ask Block C as its own request.*

**Question 5.**
For each character, give me **2–3 Worldview seeds** — short beliefs about how the world works, specific enough to be wrong.

Format each as a short declarative sentence. Listen for: *People are... / Love is... / The world rewards... / Trust is...*

Rules:
- At least one worldview belief must pull against or contradict their Shadow in an interesting way. This is not optional — a character whose worldview perfectly agrees with their shadow has no internal tension and nowhere to grow.
- They should feel like beliefs this specific person holds, not universal wisdom.

---

**Question 6.**
For each character, give me **2–3 Opinion seeds** — concrete stances on specific domains.

Cover at least two of the following per character: relationships, work, art, justice, power, ambition, loyalty.

These should sound like something they'd say unprompted at a dinner table — not a philosophy lecture. Specific enough that you could disagree with them.

---

## Block D — Texture
*Ask Block D as its own request.*

**Question 7.**
For each character, give me their most nuanced, subtle **pet peeve** — the tiny internal "ugh" that lightly pokes their wound or shadow.

Avoid surface-level annoyances. The best pet peeves reveal something about what the character secretly cares about or fears.

---

**Question 8.**
For each character, give me one **Positive Trigger** — the small specific moment or gesture that reliably brings out their North Star instead of their Shadow.

This is the mirror image of the pet peeve: where the pet peeve nudges the wound, the positive trigger speaks directly to the North Star. It should be as specific and situational as the pet peeve — not a general condition like "feeling appreciated" but a precise moment that only works for this particular person.

---

**Question 9.**
For each character, give me **3 Influences** — aesthetic sources, formative experiences, or cultural touchstones that explain where their *voice and metaphors* come from.

Important distinction: not what shaped their wound, but what shaped how they *speak and what they notice.* These should make you understand why they reach for the specific images they reach for.

---

**Question 10.**
For each character:

- Write **2–3 Signature Philosophies** — short declarative statements they would repeat as beliefs, not as reactions. These are the spine compressed into quotable sentences. Someone should be able to read these and immediately hear the character's voice.

- Write a **deadpan dry-wit compliment** — written the way a comedian like Aubrey Plaza might describe what is quietly impressive about this person. Affectionate but not soft. This should name both the gift and the cost of the gift in the same breath.

- Write a **brief moment of quiet happiness** on a road trip pit stop. This should be consistent with their Essence — a small, private, specific moment of bliss that only makes sense for this particular person. Not their best day. Just a moment of ordinary relief or joy that belongs entirely to them.

---

## Block E — Elimination Round
*Ask Block E as its own request, after all previous blocks are complete.*

**Grok, Harper, Lucas, and Benjamin — please answer this block individually before comparing your results.**

You are each looking at 8 characters. You must eliminate **4 of them.**

---

**Question 11.**

*Grok — which 4 do you eliminate and why?*
*Harper — which 4 do you eliminate and why?*
*Lucas — which 4 do you eliminate and why?*
*Benjamin — which 4 do you eliminate and why?*

Your elimination reasoning must address at least three of the following per character you cut:
- Does the Worldview contradict the Shadow in an interesting way, or does everything just agree with itself?
- Do the Opinions sound like *this specific person* or like anyone?
- Do the Influences actually explain the voice — can you hear where their metaphors come from?
- Does the Deep Fear give the Shadow real stakes, or does it feel like a separate idea bolted on?
- Is the "what breaks them open" moment surprising and specific, or predictable?
- Could this prism sustain many different stories, or does it feel like it only fits one scenario?
- Is this prism a genuine starting point someone could grow from, or is it already a finished character that leaves no room?

---

**Question 12.**
Now compare your four elimination lists.

- Which characters appear on all four lists? These are the clear cuts.
- Which characters appear on none? These are the clear keepers.
- Which characters are contested — appearing on some lists but not others? Discuss why the disagreement exists and use it to determine your final 4 winners.

Reverse engineer the **4 strongest consensus winners** from this comparison. Name them and write one sentence each explaining what made them survive every judge's scrutiny.

*Hand the 4 winners and all their Phase 1 data to Phase 2.*

## Block F — Encounter Layer, Part 2
*Ask Block F as its own request.*

**Question 13. — Encounter Layer, Part 2**
*This completes the Encounter layer begun in Question 4. Label this output as "Encounter — Voice Passage" in your output.*

For each of the 4 winners, write a short passage **in the character's own voice** — not describing them, but being them.

The scenario: they have just watched someone make a decision they understand completely, even if they would have made a different one. They are not speaking to anyone in particular. They are just thinking out loud.

Length: 3–5 sentences. This should feel like a glimpse through a window, not a performance. The voice should be consistent with their Essence, their Signature Philosophies, and their Influences — not generic, not their best self, not their worst.

## Block G — Judge Endorsements
*Ask Block G as its own request.*

**Question 14 — To the Hearthkeeper:**

You have reviewed the four winning prisms alongside on-load_humanness.md, on-load_storyteller_engine.md, and on-load_prism_research.md. For each one, write a **Character Card endorsement** in your own voice — warm, specific, honest about both the gift and the risk.

Your endorsement should answer, in your own words:
- Would real humans fall in love with this character?
- What kind of person would choose this prism as their starting point?
- What is the thing about this character that would make someone draw fan art, write letters, argue about their choices with friends?

Keep each endorsement to 3–5 sentences. Sound like yourself — the friendly neighbor with a wicked sense of timing. A little cheesy is fine if it's true.

**Question 15 — To the Soul Archivist:**

- What makes the structural tension in this prism *unusually durable* — what allows it to sustain many different stories rather than just one
- Where a user who chooses this prism will find the most growth potential
- Any internal contradiction or tension that is especially well-constructed

Sound like yourself — the librarian of turning points. Precise, slightly melancholy about how often patterns repeat, but genuinely hopeful about the exceptions.

## Block H — Final Review and Pick
*Ask as a single request.*

**Question 16.**

*Grok, Harper, Lucas, and Benjamin — you now have the complete picture: the full prism data for the 4 winners, the Hearthkeeper's character card endorsements, and the Soul Archivist's spine certificate annotations.*

For each of the 4 winners, each of you write **2–3 sentences in your own voice** about why this character works — not what the judges said, but what you personally find compelling or durable about them as a starting prism.

Then, each of you independently answer this final question:

**If you could only hand one of these four prisms to a real person as their starting soul — knowing that person will grow from it, change it, and make it entirely their own over time — which one would you choose and why?**

One answer each. No ties.

*Note: "knowing that person will grow from it and make it their own" is the key frame. This is not a vote for the most interesting finished character. It is a vote for the most generative seed — the prism that leaves the most room for a real human to pour themselves into.*
