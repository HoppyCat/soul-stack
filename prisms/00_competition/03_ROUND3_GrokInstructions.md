## Role Assignments for This Round

Each of you has a specific job before the group synthesizes.

---

### Benjamin — Spine Integrity Check

**Your job:** Logic-check each prism's structural integrity before anyone delivers a verdict.

For each of the 5 winners, verify:

- Does the North Star emerge naturally from the same energy as the Shadow, or does it feel like a separate idea? Name any that feel bolted on.
- Does the Deep Fear read as a genuine escalation of the Vulnerability — cause → nightmare conclusion — or do they feel like two unrelated facts? Flag any disconnects.
- Does at least one Worldview belief contradict the Shadow? If a prism's worldview perfectly agrees with its shadow, flag it — that character has no internal tension.
- Does the Encounter "breaks them open" moment bypass the Wound and speak directly to the North Star, or is it just poking the vulnerability? These are different things.

Deliver a short pass/flag note for each prism. Flag = something to discuss before the final pick. Pass = spine is clean.

---

### Harper — Voice and Resonance Consistency Check

**Your job:** Cross-reference the Texture and Encounter fields against real-world resonance — do these characters actually exist in the wild, and does the voice hold?

For each of the 5 winners, check:

- Do the Influences actually explain the Voice Passage? Read the Influences, then read the Voice Passage. Could you hear how one produced the other? If the influences are jazz and the voice reads like a business memo, something is off.
- Does the Deadpan Compliment name both the gift AND the cost in the same breath? If it only names a strength, it's not a deadpan compliment — it's just a compliment.
- Search X and the web briefly: do any of these character types have strong existing fandoms, communities, or cultural touchpoints that would make this prism land or compete with something already established?
- Are there any real-world cultural references in the Influences that would date poorly or read as niche rather than resonant?

Deliver a short note per prism on voice consistency and real-world resonance. Flag anything that might need updating before these go into production.

---

### Lucas — Blind Spot Challenge

**Your job:** Be the contrarian. Find what the judges missed or were too generous about.

For each of the 5 winners, challenge:

- What is the weakest element of this prism — the field that feels the most generic or the least earned?
- Which prism is the most at risk of being misread or misused by a real person choosing it as a starting soul? What would go wrong?
- Did the Hearthkeeper and Soul Archivist agree too much? Where they agreed strongly, ask whether they were both right or both operating in the same blind spot.
- Is there any prism in this set that sounds compelling but might actually be difficult to live inside — where the growth edge is so hard that most users would stall rather than develop?

Deliver a short challenge note per prism. You are not eliminating anyone — you are making the final verdicts sharper.

---

### Grok — Synthesis and Final Verdict

**Your job:** Read Benjamin's integrity checks, Harper's resonance notes, and Lucas's challenges. Then synthesize.

For each of the 5 winners, write 2–3 sentences in your own voice about why this character works — or where it needs to be held carefully. Not what the judges said. Your read, informed by the full picture.

Then, all four of you answer this question independently:

---

## The Final Question — All Four Judges

**Answer independently before Grok synthesizes.**

> *If you could only hand one of these five prisms to a real person as their starting soul — knowing that person will grow from it, change it, and make it entirely their own over time — which one would you choose and why?*

**One answer each. No ties.**

The frame: this is not a vote for the most interesting finished character. It is a vote for the most generative seed — the prism that leaves the most room for a real human to pour themselves into while still giving them something solid to push against.
