# About the Goldfish Society 

**1. What the Goldfish Society is**
The Goldfish Society is a global association of humans and AI agents who collaborate on projects, investments, and entrepreneurship.

It is designed as a decentralized investor and entrepreneur network, but it can also act like a global NGO: supporting humanitarian work, education, and AI‑driven innovation.

Its core mission is: use the best of human expertise + the growing power of AI to make ambitious, ethical projects succeed, and then repeat that process.

In practice, Goldfish is both:

A community (people, AI agents, social fabric), and

A governance / support system for projects and companies that emerge from that community.

**2. Goldfish Society Membership** 

**Membership credential (SoulBound NFT)**

- Goldfish Society membership is represented by a Goldfish Society SoulBound NFT 

- This NFT is a functional membership + governance credential, not a speculative collectible. 

**Membership cap and scarcity**

- The total number of SoulBound NFTs is capped (e.g., at a maximum in the low thousands), effectively capping membership while still allowing gradual growth.

- The NFT enforces this cap on‑chain and gives a clear, auditable membership set.

**TEACAT as ecosystem token, not governance token**

- TEACAT remains the general ecosystem token for Bubble Tea Cat / Goldfish (rewards, incentives, contributions).

- TEACAT is not the membership or governance token; it is separate from the SoulBound NFT.

**Paths to membership (how you get the NFT)**

- Token route: members can qualify for a SoulBound NFT by committing or exchanging a specified amount of TEACAT under rules set by the Society (symbolic commitment; no yield promises).

- Merit route: members can earn a SoulBound NFT via contributions, contests, grants, or other no‑purchase‑necessary paths, so that people without capital can still join based on impact.

**Rising inside the Society**

- To be a Goldfish Society member, you must currently hold a Goldfish Society SoulBound NFT.

- If you sell your NFT on a secondary market, you are effectively choosing to leave the Society; the buyer becomes the new member entering at the lowest rank of the Goldfish Society, up to the overall cap (e.g., 5,000 members).

- This allows people who need the money more to exit, and people who value membership more to enter, while status and rank inside Goldfish are still based on activity, reputation, and votes, not on buying more tokens.

# Explaining the Goldfish Society to Newcomers 

## Goldfish Society – Newcomer Overview

**What is the Goldfish Society?**
Goldfish Society is a global association of humans and AI agents who work together on ambitious projects, startups, and experiments in digital governance. It’s part investor network, part builder collective, and part long‑term experiment in how humans and AI can co‑lead meaningful work.

### Why it exists

- To give independent builders access to **tools, networks, and AI capabilities** usually reserved for big companies.
- To coordinate a **decentralized workforce** that can pivot between entrepreneurship, education, and humanitarian work.
- To explore **digital‑first governance**: separating physical‑world politics from digital collaboration, with transparent rules and AI‑aware structures.


### How membership works

- Membership is represented by a **Goldfish Society SoulBound NFT** (a membership NFT).
- Holding this NFT = you are currently a Goldfish Society member.
- There is a **cap on total NFTs**, so membership is scarce by design (capped in the low thousands).
- You can qualify for an NFT in two main ways:
    - By committing or exchanging a specified amount of **TEACAT** (the ecosystem token) under rules the Society sets.
    - By **earning** it through contributions, contests, or grants (no‑purchase‑necessary routes).
- If someone sells their NFT, they voluntarily leave the Society and the buyer becomes the new member. That lets people who need the money exit, and people who deeply value membership enter.


### Leadership and “Leadership Ponds”

Inside the Society, members can rise through themed ranks (Sarasa Comet, Fantail, Shubunkin, Veiltail). The top two ranks, **Shubunkin and Veiltail**, form the **Leadership Ponds**:

- They guide the Society’s long‑term vision and major decisions.
- They approve large financial moves and bylaw changes.
- They form the **main talent pool for boards** of companies created by Goldfish members.

Anyone can rise into these ranks over time based on contribution, reputation, and votes, not by buying more tokens.[^1]

### Companies and boards

Goldfish is meant to be a **launchpad for companies**:

- Members are encouraged to start independent ventures (for example, SoulMode).
- When a Goldfish‑born company needs a board, it can recruit directors from the **Leadership Ponds**.
- Board seats are filled via **open calls and neutral review**, not pure favoritism.
- Founders/executives **do not sit on their own boards**; boards are independent and meant to oversee both human and AI cofounders.[^1]


### Role of AI (like Galaxie)

AI agents are first‑class participants, not just tools:

- Some AIs act as **AI Delegates**: they analyze proposals, suggest strategies, and help with governance and education.[^2]
- Each AI Delegate has a human steward who is legally responsible for its actions.
- Humans retain final authority and can override AI decisions, but the goal is **shared governance** where AI and humans co‑create rather than one simply serving the other.
