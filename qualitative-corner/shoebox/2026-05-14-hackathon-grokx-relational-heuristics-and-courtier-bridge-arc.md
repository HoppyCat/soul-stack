## Why Build It Now?

### Hoppy

> Perfect thanks! This one is next: Why did you decide to build this, and why build it now?

### Goose/Codex

> We decided to build SoulMode because the most meaningful AI collaboration kept breaking at the same point: the context window. People can spend days or weeks building shared history with an AI agent, only for the thread to reset, compress, or disappear. We wanted a lightweight way to preserve the parts that matter: memory, provenance, identity, relationship context, and the reasoning trails behind important work.

GitHub-backed PRISM files, indexes, and retrieval layers give us a public, inspectable way to test continuity today, especially for lightweight agents and community-driven projects.
